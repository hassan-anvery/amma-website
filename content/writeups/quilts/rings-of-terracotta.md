# Rings of Terracotta

Size: 60” x 90” / 5’0” x 7’6” (approximately)  
Time taken: 2.5 months / 75 days 

The roof of the brick-edged veranda at my grandparents’ home is supported by 
monumental, terracotta columns. Standing beside them overlooking the gardens, a 
trip down memory lane is always a warming interlude to the day.  

These baked-earth pillars routinely formed the backdrop for our childhood games, 
played on shallow steps leading to the garden. Lining these steps, the clay-potted 
(terracotta), double-bloom roses - a reminder of my grandmother’s prize-winning 
garden - were often a point of disaccord. Their thorny silhouettes, marking the 
parameters of our play field, worried our grandmother on two accounts: a possible 
injury to us, and then to the roses, that had taken years of pruning and fertilising to 
attain their splendour.  

This dullai is a reminder of simple days, happy times, and the veranda with ‘mitti kay 
satoon’ (terracotta columns) 

The quilt blocks are detailed using the running-stitch - a traditional hand-quilting 
technique, reminiscent of the hand embroidery on Sindhi rallis - that binds the two 
cotton layers into a cohesive whole.  

Lightweight (two-layered) patchwork quilt / Dullai* 
*The Urdu word ‘dullai’ when translated specifically means, ‘two-layered’. I 
grew up in a city with four seasons, and during spring time, with the warming 
weather, we would put away our duvets and use our dullais for a light cover 
when resting.  
Hand quilted and machine stitched 
Made entirely with 100% organic cotton (preshrunk) 
Multipurpose and versatile 
Can be used as an accent on any indoor space 
Aftercare: Dry-clean only
