# Daniyaal's Childhood - A Visual Narrative

A keepsake quilt made mostly of Daniyaal's baby clothes, with a few of his parents' 
clothes. 

Size: 54" x 75" (approximately) 
Time taken: approximately 2.5 months. 

Little Daniyaal is all grown up now - turning 22 years old and about to graduate from 
university. 

Endless gratitude to Daniyaal's parents for giving me complete freedom to design 
with no time constraints. Above all for sharing those precious memories depicted on 
this quilt. I have had so much fun working on this project.  

The reverse side of the quilt has all his mother’s outfits that she wore while expecting 
Daniyaal; along with the reverse side of Garfield (placed right behind its front image; 
and all the sweet little pockets from his baby clothes. These pockets are all usable, 
and little notes can be put into them!  
 
Stories of Daniyaal depicted on this quilt:  
 
Born on the 25th of September 2004, Daniyaal is the eldest of three siblings 

A mother's dream baby, who slept really well - 3 hours in the afternoon and 12 hours 
in the night. 

In his mother's words, "Daniyaal loved King Kong (the first full movie that he probably 
watched) – possibly because it also contained his favourite dinosaurs. The 
obsession with rewatching lasted a long time, and through this phase, Daniyaal liked 
to re-enact the King Kong vs Dinosaur fight scene with his father".  

The tree on this panel is made from Daniyaal's overalls. The foliage of the trees is 
made from his little baby onesie that had the camouflage print. The green leaves, the 
red flowers, and the yellow grass are from his mother's clothes 

Was a boy scout for a few years in middle school. Took part in Scouts Jamboree in 
Nova Scotia. Also had many funny (often exaggerated      ) stories from his various 
camps. One of them is depicted on this quilt - in his mother's words: "having to sleep 
in the tent during winter survival camp, and waking up with his feet sticking in a pool 
of water (melted ice) - while miraculously not getting any visible frost bite to validate 
this story". 

Loved drawing dinosaurs and dragons.  

Crazy about gaming. Daniyaal has maintained friendships through gaming (Call of 
Duty specifically), despite numerous relocations in the last decade. 

Used to love ‘The Incredibles’ (that he referred to as “tedibles”) and for a while called 
his father “Bob” instead of “Baba”, since that was Mr Incredible’s name.  

Discovered basketball, during covid, and it really helped Daniyaal deal with the 
severe lockdowns in Manila. For him, sports has often been the best way for meeting 
new people and making friends. Basketball has also helped him connect with others 
at university. 

During the years the family was in Saudi, little Daniyaal enjoyed playing in the 
compound with his friends - riding bikes, playing soccer and eating Burger King 
together. 

A thrill-seeker. Enjoyed zip-lining, roller coasters, high speed/elevation rides - "a 
surprise for an otherwise very prudent/risk-averse child". 

Loved Messi. Lived and breathed soccer. Had deep analytical knowledge about both 
the game and the players. At one time wanted to grow up and be a professional 
soccer coach. 

In his mother's words: "Super analytical. Would refuse to pick up a book to read but 
would devour the news since he was 9 years old (downloaded a BBC news app on 
his ipad), and most of the time seemed to know more about the world around him 
than most adults in his life did." 

Daniyaal started talking when he was 11 months old. Some phrases/words that his 
mother remembers from his younger years are: 'Apoo Pie' (Apple pie), 'Two Toffies' 
(request from grandparents every time they stepped out), and 'Epic' 

Had a long-lasting nutella love, from childhood to teenagers, but it did not last 
beyond that 

Little Daniyaal had two strong obsessions: Trucks - could name different kinds, by 
the time he was 2; and Dinosaurs - aged 4 - had lots of dinosaur toys and books
